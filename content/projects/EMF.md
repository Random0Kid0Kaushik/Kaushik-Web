The EMF Corporate Website is basically the face of the company online, showcasing everything from our services and operations to our sustainability efforts and latest news. It’s the go-to place for customers, partners, regulators, and anyone else interested in what we do in marine fuel logistics and digital bunkering.
I teamed up with another developer on this website overhaul project. We focused on giving the existing site a fresh look and feel, aiming to enhance user experience, boost performance, and keep content relevant. Our goal was to make sure visitors could easily find up-to-date info about the company, recent news, and our digital projects while also positioning EMF as a forward-thinking and tech-savvy organization.
The project kicked off to fix some issues with the old website. It had outdated content, slow update processes, and design limits that didn’t showcase our latest business developments well. With the new site, EMF wanted to enhance its brand image, improve communication with stakeholders, and create a modern, responsive experience for all users.
We built the website using WordPress as our main content management system, along with custom development work mainly in HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. At first, we used Elementor for quick layout prototyping, but we had to ditch it later on because it was slowing things down. So, we switched to more direct coding methods to improve performance and flexibility.
Some key features of the site include pages about the company, sections on sustainability and digital transformation, as well as news and service overview pages. We made sure the site works well on both desktops and mobile devices, so it’s accessible to a wide audience. Plus, it’s easier for internal teams to update content, keeping everything fresh without needing major redevelopment.
During my time on this project, I helped develop website content and create new pages for the revamped site. I focused on turning designs into functionally working web pages, ensuring everything looked consistent, was responsive, and met design standards. Through this experience, I improved my skills in HTML, CSS, and JavaScript, learned how to convert Figma design prototypes into ready-to-use web components, and gained valuable experience collaborating with a development team to launch a fully functional website.
